About the Tour

Gardens wild and manicured,
big and small

Camp Hill residents — within and beyond the borough — have opened their gardens to inspire you. Discover how any size or style of garden can sustain our critical pollinators: bees, butterflies, and birds alike. This year's mid-summer date lets us see what's in peak bloom for pollinators in July.

Date Sunday, July 12 1:00 – 4:00 PM
First Stop Bee City Pollinator Garden 2145 Walnut St, Camp Hill

What to Expect

Your afternoon, three simple steps

I

Collect your booklet

Begin at the Bee City Pollinator Garden beside the Borough Building on Walnut Street. Pick up your tour map and guide — and enter the free raffle.

II

Explore at your pace

Visit as many gardens as you like between 1 and 4 PM. Every resident gardener will be on hand to answer questions and share what they've learned.

III

Take home ideas

Leave inspired to create or expand your own pollinator habitat — whether it's a window box of native flowers or a full backyard wildflower meadow.

A Sneak Peek

Explore the Gardens

Every garden tells a story. From native plant sanctuaries and pollinator habitats to colorful cottage gardens and productive edible landscapes, this year's tour offers a unique look at how Camp Hill residents are creating beautiful, thriving outdoor spaces. Here's a sneak peek at what you'll discover along the way.

Native Habitat Haven

Discover a vibrant suburban oasis filled with native plants, pollinator favorites, and year-round wildlife habitat. See how one homeowner transformed a traditional landscape into a thriving ecosystem.

A Pollinator Paradise

Buzzing with bees, butterflies, and hummingbirds, this garden showcases the power of native plants to support local wildlife while creating a peaceful backyard retreat.

A Park-Like Retreat

Stroll through a garden inspired by generations of family gardeners, featuring flowering shrubs, water features, and a landscape designed to feel like a neighborhood park.

From Lawn to Living Landscape

Explore a front yard transformed from a traditional lawn into a flourishing native plant garden with more than 50 species supporting pollinators and improving local habitat.

A Sanctuary for Wildlife

Created with birds, bees, and butterflies in mind, this garden is packed with biodiversity, colorful blooms, and native plants that keep the landscape alive with activity.

Stewardship in Bloom

See how one family blends pollinator habitat, edible herbs, and native flowers to create a beautiful landscape while teaching the next generation about caring for the earth.

An English Garden Inspired Escape

A mix of classic garden charm and pollinator-friendly plantings awaits, featuring vibrant flowers, native shrubs, and seasonal color around every corner.

The Garden Next Door

Backing up to Willow Park, this charming garden features flowering borders, climbing vines, fruit trees, and vegetable gardens inspired by family traditions.

An Edible Garden Wonderland

Packed with fruits, vegetables, herbs, and pollinator plants, this impressive garden demonstrates just how much food can be grown in a small suburban space.

Butterflies Welcome

A colorful garden designed to support butterflies through every stage of life, featuring host plants, pollinator-friendly blooms, and both sun and shade garden spaces.

A Color Lover's Garden

From spring bulbs to summer dahlias and fall blooms, this garden celebrates color throughout the seasons with a stunning collection of flowers, shrubs, and vegetables.

Girl Scouts for Pollinators

Created by a local Girl Scout troop, this thriving community garden showcases how young environmental stewards are helping bees, butterflies, and other pollinators flourish.

About the Host

Camp Hill Bee City USA

Camp Hill is proud to be recognized as a Bee City USA community, committed to protecting pollinators and creating healthier habitats for bees, butterflies, hummingbirds, and other essential wildlife.

The Bee City USA Committee is made up of dedicated community volunteers working to expand pollinator-friendly habitats throughout Camp Hill, promote native plantings, and raise awareness about pollinator conservation. Join us in celebrating our community's commitment to a more vibrant and sustainable environment for both people and pollinators.
