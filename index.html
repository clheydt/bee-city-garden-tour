About the Tour

Gardens wild and manicured,
big and small

Camp Hill residents — within and beyond the borough — have opened their gardens to inspire you. Discover how any size or style of garden can sustain our critical pollinators: bees, butterflies, and birds alike. This year's mid-summer date lets us see what's in peak bloom for pollinators in July.

Date Sunday, July 12 1:00 – 4:00 PM
First Stop Bee City Pollinator Garden 2145 Walnut St, Camp Hill
Format Self-Guided Tour at your leisure
Cost Free No registration required
What to Expect

Your afternoon, three simple steps

I

Collect your booklet

Begin at the Bee City Pollinator Garden beside the Borough Building on Walnut Street. Pick up your tour map and guide — and enter the free raffle.

II

Explore at your pace

Visit as many gardens as you like between 1 and 4 PM. Every resident gardener will be on hand to answer questions and share what they've learned.

III

Take home ideas

Leave inspired to create or expand your own pollinator habitat — whether it's a window box of native flowers or a full backyard wildflower meadow.

About the Host

Camp Hill Bee City USA

Camp Hill is proud to be recognized as a Bee City USA community, committed to protecting pollinators and creating healthier habitats for bees, butterflies, hummingbirds, and other essential wildlife.

The Bee City USA Committee is made up of dedicated community volunteers working to expand pollinator-friendly habitats throughout Camp Hill, promote native plantings, and raise awareness about pollinator conservation. Join us in celebrating our community's commitment to a more vibrant and sustainable environment for both people and pollinators.
